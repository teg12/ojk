What is plagiarism?

Plagiarizing is when you present someone else's ideas/work as your own without their consent or without citing them as a source. There are many forms of plagiarism and terms like "copying" and "borrowing" can be used to disguise the severity of the offence. There are many examples of plagiarism for example; to steal someone else's ideas and using it as your own, to use someone's information without citing them as a source, reusing an existing document, to copy someone else's document, etc... More examples of plagiarism can include copying words or ideas from someone else without giving them credit, not adding quotation marks and copying a sentence structure and changing the words without giving credit. them as a source.
